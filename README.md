## Projects

### Project 1 — RetailCo Customer Analytics
**Domain:** E-Commerce | **Tools:** SQL · Power BI · Excel · draw.io · Confluence · Jira

A UK online retailer with a 30% customer churn rate and no visibility of which customers were at risk. I ran the discovery, wrote the BRD, mapped the current and target-state processes in BPMN, ran SQL analysis across the customer dataset, and defined the requirements for a Power BI dashboard to surface at-risk customer segments to the retention team.

**Artefacts in this repo:** BRD v1.0 · SQL analysis (5 modules) · Customer, orders and products datasets

**Key finding:** Bronze-tier customers were churning at ~80%. The loyalty programme was spending money acquiring customers who left almost immediately. The SQL RFM analysis identified 4 active customers needing immediate outreach.

📂 [`project-1-retailco/`](./project-1-retailco/)

### Project 2 — PayFlow Mobile Payment Feature
**Domain:** Fintech | **Tools:** Jira · Confluence · draw.io · Excel

A UK fintech startup with 180,000 users losing 34% of customers at checkout because every payment redirected to an external browser. I defined the end-to-end requirements for a native in-app payment feature — including Stripe API integration specs, PCI-DSS compliance requirements, a full Jira backlog (3 Epics, 12 Stories, 28 Subtasks), and the UAT test plan.

**Key decision:** Identified that using Stripe Elements removes PayFlow from PCI-DSS scope entirely — saving an estimated 6 months of compliance overhead. That was a requirements decision, not an architecture one.

📂 [`project-2-payflow/`](./project-2-payflow/)

---

### Project 3 — InsureRight Digital Transformation
**Domain:** Insurance / Financial Services | **Tools:** Tableau · Excel · draw.io · Confluence

A UK insurance firm processing 1,200 claims a month on paper forms and email chains. Average processing time: 14 days. Industry benchmark: 5 days. I facilitated the elicitation workshop, mapped the current process (6 bottlenecks), produced the gap analysis across people, process and technology, applied MoSCoW to 24 requirements, and built a Tableau dashboard to present the business case to the board.

**Key finding:** During process walkthroughs with frontline staff, I found 4 unofficial Excel trackers that management didn't know existed. Those spreadsheets became the clearest evidence of how broken the current system was — and the centrepiece of the business case.

📂 [`project-3-insureright/`](./project-3-insureright/)
