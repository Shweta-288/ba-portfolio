# Project 3 — InsureRight Digital Transformation

**Domain:** Insurance / Financial Services | **Duration:** 10 weeks | **Status:** Complete

**Tools:** Tableau · Excel · draw.io · Confluence

---

## The Problem

InsureRight processes 1,200 insurance claims a month — every single one starting with a paper form. Average processing time: 14 days. UK industry benchmark: 5 days. Staff were spending 40% of their working time on manual data re-entry. 18% of claims required rework due to errors introduced in the manual process.

The business had approved a digital transformation budget but needed a BA to define the current state, quantify the gaps, and build the business case before any system was selected.

## What I Did

I facilitated the elicitation workshop, but the most useful thing I did happened before it. I spent half a day doing process walkthroughs with two frontline Claims Handlers — and found four unofficial Excel spreadsheets they'd built to compensate for the things the legacy system couldn't do. Management didn't know these existed. They became the centrepiece of the business case.

The workshop surfaced conflicting root cause theories: the Operations Director thought it was a people and training problem, the IT Lead thought the system was broken, the Claims Manager thought it was a process design issue. All three were partly right. I ran a 5 Whys analysis in the room using the observed evidence from the walkthroughs, which shifted the conversation from debate to diagnosis.

The board didn't want a requirements document — they wanted to know if the investment was worth it. I built the Tableau dashboard specifically to answer that: cost of inaction (£1.09M a year), estimated investment (£320K), payback period (under six months). The budget was approved.

## What I Produced

- Elicitation workshop notes: requirements log (24 items), assumption register (11 items), stakeholder feedback summary
- BPMN AS-IS map: 6 bottlenecks documented, undocumented workarounds captured
- BPMN TO-BE map: digitised 7-step workflow with automated routing and finance system integration
- Gap analysis across three dimensions: People, Process, Technology
- MoSCoW requirements: 24 items across 4 tiers
- Tableau dashboard: business case visualisation for board presentation
- Benefits Realisation Plan: 5 KPIs with owners and measurement timelines
- Risk Register: 8 risks with likelihood, impact, and mitigation strategies
- Change Impact Assessment
